Football terms, explained

Every phrase you'll hear from commentary, the dressing room, and the terrace -- broken down in plain language, grouped by where you'll actually hear it used.

1' Glossary
01

Kick-off

The pass that starts each half, and restarts play after a goal, taken from the centre spot.

02

Clean sheet

When a goalkeeper or team finishes a match without conceding a single goal.

03

Hat-trick

Three goals scored by the same player in a single match.

04

Nutmeg

Passing or dribbling the ball through an opponent's legs, considered a small humiliation on the pitch.

05

Offside

An attacker is offside if they're nearer the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is played to them.

06

False nine

A forward who drops deep out of the striker position to create space and pull defenders out of shape.

07

High press

A team pressuring the opposition high up the pitch to win the ball back quickly, rather than sitting off.

08

Overlap

A full-back or wing-back running past a winger on the outside to create an extra passing option down the flank.

09

Low block

A team defending with most players packed close to their own goal, conceding space further up the pitch.

10

Formation

The shape a team lines up in, usually described by rows of defenders, midfielders and forwards, such as 4-3-3.

11

Yellow card

A caution shown for a reckless foul, dissent, or unsporting behaviour. A second yellow becomes a red.

12

Red card

Immediate dismissal from the match for serious foul play, denying a clear goal-scoring chance, or violent conduct.

13

VAR

Video Assistant Referee -- match officials reviewing footage to check goals, penalties, red cards and cases of mistaken identity.

14

Professional foul

A deliberate foul, often to stop a promising attack, accepted as a tactical cost rather than an accident.

15

Advantage

When a referee lets play continue after a foul because the fouled team is still in a better position without a stoppage.

16

Group stage

The opening phase of a competition where teams are split into small groups and play each other before knockout rounds begin.

17

Away goals

A rule, once common in two-legged ties, where goals scored away from home could break a tie on aggregate.

18

Relegation

The drop of a club from one league division to a lower one, usually after finishing among the bottom teams in a season.

19

Aggregate score

The combined score across two matches in a tie, most commonly home and away legs in cup competitions.

20

Silverware

A general term for trophies won by a club across league and cup competitions.
